# Breach Notification Email — Template

**Document owner:** Data Protection Officer
**Contact:** dpo@gighala.my
**Version:** 1.0

> **USAGE INSTRUCTIONS**
>
> 1. Fill in all `[BRACKETED]` fields before sending.
> 2. Have the DPO and Legal review the draft before it goes out.
> 3. Send from: `privacy@gighala.my` (not a no-reply address — users must be able to reply).
> 4. Subject line must not be alarming but must be clear. Do not use "breach" in the subject if legal advises against it; use "Important Notice Regarding Your Account Data".
> 5. Send BCC to `dpo@gighala.my` for the notification log.
> 6. Log the send in the Incident Report (Step 6 of SOP).

---

## Template A — Full Breach (IC/Passport Data Involved)

*Use when: IC numbers, IC images, or passport data may have been exposed.*

---

**FROM:** GigHala Privacy Team `<privacy@gighala.my>`
**TO:** `[user registered email]`
**SUBJECT:** Important Notice: Your GigHala Account Data

---

Dear [Full Name / GigHala username],

We are writing to inform you of a security incident that may have affected your personal data on GigHala.

**What happened**

On [DATE], we discovered that [BRIEF PLAIN-LANGUAGE DESCRIPTION — e.g., "an unauthorised party gained temporary access to a portion of our database"]. We took immediate action to stop the access and have since secured the affected systems.

**What data was involved**

The following information associated with your account may have been accessed:

- [x] Full name
- [x] IC / MyKad number
- [x] IC / Passport images uploaded for identity verification
- [ ] Email address
- [ ] Phone number
- [ ] Bank account information

*(Remove items that do not apply.)*

We do not store card numbers or payment credentials — all payments are processed by our payment provider.

**What we have done**

- Contained and stopped the unauthorised access as of [DATE/TIME]
- Notified the Department of Personal Data Protection (JPDP) as required
- Engaged a cybersecurity team to investigate the root cause
- Strengthened access controls and rotated system credentials
- Are conducting a full security audit

**What you should do**

Because your IC/MyKad number may have been involved, we strongly recommend taking the following steps:

1. **Lodge a police report** — Visit your nearest police station and report that your IC details may have been compromised. Request a copy of the report for your records.

2. **Alert your bank** — Contact your bank(s) and inform them that your IC number may have been exposed. Ask them to flag your account for suspicious activity.

3. **Monitor your credit** — Check your CCRIS report via Bank Negara's eCCRIS portal (eccris.bnm.gov.my) for any unfamiliar credit applications made in your name.

4. **Be cautious of phishing** — You may receive emails or calls claiming to be from government agencies or banks. Do not provide personal information over the phone or via unsolicited links.

5. **Change your GigHala password** — As a precaution, please change your password at gighala.com/settings.

**Your rights under PDPA 2010**

Under the Personal Data Protection Act 2010, you have the right to:
- Access the personal data we hold about you
- Request correction of inaccurate data
- Contact us with any questions or concerns

To exercise these rights, email `privacy@gighala.my` with the subject line "PDPA Data Request".

**Contact us**

We sincerely apologise for this incident and the concern it may cause. If you have any questions, please contact our Data Protection Officer directly:

- **Email:** dpo@gighala.my
- **Subject:** Data Breach Inquiry — [your username or registered email]

We will respond within 3 business days.
